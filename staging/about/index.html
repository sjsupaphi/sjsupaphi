History

The fraternity of Pi Alpha Phi was founded in 1929 at the University of California, Berkely by six individuals: D. Wing Tom, Wing Chan, Elemer Leung, Chack Chan, Tim Jang and George Lee. During this era, the founding fathers of Pi Alpha Phi along with many other Asian-Americans faced discrimination in various forms. Student lodging was often not rented to those of Asian heritage, it was the goodwill of a sympathetic German woman known as Mother Tusch, who had also been a victim of racism, that gave the founding fathers Wing Chan, Elmer Leong, and D. Wing Tom housing in Mother Tusch's cabin behind her house. Traditional Greek fraternities also legally discriminated against men of color, having explicit policies prohibiting the membership of nonwhites and non-Christians. It was during this time that people of different ethnic backgrounds - namely Latino, Black and Asian - began creating their own Greek organizations. It was these forms of racial discrimination that pushed the six founding fathers of Pi Alpha Phi together in which they conceived the idea to form a fraternity to serve the several hundred students of Asian descent at the University of California, Berkely. On February 1st, 1929, the six Founding Fathers oficially signed the fraternity's constitution and established Pi Alpha Phi at the University of California, Berkely.

Our Pillars

Written in the original constituion are several values that Pi Alpha Phi aims to instill and maintain in all its members.

Brotherhood

Pi Alpha Phi is meant to be an extended family that nurtures life-long friendships through bonds of tradition and shared values. Among our pillars, Brotherhood is Pi Alpha Phi's immutable asset.

Leadership

Pi Alpha Phi fosters leadership by providing opportunities to fulfill important duties within the organization in preperation for our Brothers' future careers and endeavors.

Asian Awareness

Pi Alpha Phi believes in learning Asian American culture, heritage, and history. The fraternity encourages its members to learn and pass this knowledge on to others.

Academic Excellence

Pi Alpha Phi encourages all of its members to achieve academic excellence and nurtures a studious environement for our brothers to succeed.

Philanthropy

Pi Alpha Phi believes in a responsbility to serve others and encourages participation in activties for the betterment of the community. The fraternity's national philanthropy is the Jade Ribbon Campaign.
